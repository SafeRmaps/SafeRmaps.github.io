The Problem

Every few months lately, the world is reminded of the uncertainty in each of our lives in the form of refugees and displaced persons generated due to conflict, and now even due to the changing climate.

Families are stripped of their resources, left hungry, thirsty, and often assaulted and injured to fend for their own and loved ones' lives. Chances of survival in their hometown become drastically low and force them to require relocation.

Millions of such families exist worldwide, and thousands die migrating each year due to malnutrition, infectious but preventable diseases, and violence. And these numbers only seem to be growing each year.

The tools refugees currently have to rely on are conflict profiteers and non-state actors. These refugees hand in all their documentation and trust them blindly, eventually finding themselves illegally trafficked to nearby countries in inhuman conditions.
